# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

A regression analysis was conducted to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Both simple and multiple regression models were evaluated to understand the impact of different business variables on sales performance.

The final multiple regression model achieved an R Square of 0.8114, indicating that it explains approximately 81.14% of the variation in monthly sales. This makes it the most reliable model developed during the analysis.

---

## Key Findings

The regression analysis identified the following variables as the strongest predictors of monthly sales:

* Customer Footfall – The strongest positive predictor of monthly sales.
* Marketing Spend – Higher marketing investment is associated with increased sales.
* Inventory Availability Percentage – Better product availability contributes to higher sales.
* East Region – Stores in the East region recorded lower sales than the reference region after accounting for other variables.

The North and West regional dummy variables were not statistically significant and should not be interpreted as having a meaningful impact on sales.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the regression analysis, leadership should prioritize the following actions:

* Increase customer footfall through targeted marketing campaigns and promotional activities.
* Continue investing in marketing initiatives that demonstrate measurable returns.
* Improve inventory planning to ensure products remain consistently available.
* Investigate the lower sales performance of stores in the East region to identify operational or market-specific challenges.
* Monitor store performance regularly using data-driven decision-making.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Although the model included regional dummy variables, the North and West regions were not statistically significant. Therefore, business decisions should not be based solely on these regional differences without additional supporting evidence.

---

## Risks and Limitations

The regression model provides strong predictive performance but has several limitations:

* Regression identifies statistical associations rather than cause-and-effect relationships.
* Additional factors such as customer service quality, local competition, seasonal demand, store management, and economic conditions were not included in the model.
* Stores with large residual values indicate that other business factors may also influence sales performance.

---

## Association Does Not Imply Causation

Regression analysis identifies relationships between variables but does not prove that one variable directly causes changes in another.

For example, higher marketing spend is associated with higher sales, but this does not necessarily mean that increasing marketing expenditure alone will always increase sales. Other factors such as product quality, pricing strategy, customer preferences, and market conditions may also contribute to business performance.

---

## Final Conclusion

The Multiple Regression Model is recommended as the final analytical model because it explains over 81% of the variation in monthly sales while considering multiple business drivers simultaneously.

Leadership should focus on increasing customer footfall, maintaining effective marketing investments, and improving inventory availability to support sustainable sales growth. These findings can help guide strategic planning and improve operational decision-making across retail stores.
